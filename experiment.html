Experiment No.1: To Determine Total Hardness of Water Sample in PPM

Objective: To determine the total hardness of a water sample in parts per million (ppm) using EDTA as a titrant.

Description: In this experiment, the hardness of a water sample is determined by titrating it with a standard EDTA solution. The total hardness is caused by dissolved calcium and magnesium ions, and the amount of EDTA used helps calculate the hardness in ppm.

Calculation:

The total hardness in ppm is calculated using the formula:

            Hardness (ppm) = (Volume of EDTA used x Concentration of EDTA x 1000) / Volume of Water Sample

Experiment No. 2: Determination of Mixed Alkalinity in a Water Sample

Objective: To determine the mixed alkalinity (hydroxide, carbonate, and bicarbonate) in a given water sample using titration with phenolphthalein and methyl orange as indicators.

Calculation:

The alkalinity is calculated based on the volume of acid used in the titration. The formula is as follows: 

            P-Alkalinity = (Volume of Acid x Normality of Acid x 1000) / Volume of Sample
            M-Alkalinity = (Volume of Acid x Normality of Acid x 1000) / Volume of Sample

Experiment No. 3: Cleveland Open-Cup Apparatus Experiment

Aim: To determine the flash point and fire point of petroleum products and other flammable liquids using the Cleveland Open-Cup Apparatus.

Calculation:

The flash point and fire point are calculated based on the temperatures at which the liquid ignites and maintains combustion. The formula is as follows:

            Flash Point (°C) = Measured Temperature
            Fire Point (°C) = Measured Temperature

Experiment No. 4: Pensky-Martens Closed-Cup Flash Point Calculation

Aim: To determine the flash point of volatile substances in a closed environment.

Calculation:

The flash point is determined based on the time and temperature change during the heating process. The formula is:

            Flash Point (°C) = Initial Temperature + (Heating Rate x Time to Flash)

Experiment No. 5: Moisture Content in Coal Sample Calculation

Aim: To determine the moisture content in a coal sample using proximate analysis.

Calculation:

The moisture content in the coal sample is calculated using the following formula:

            Moisture Content (%) = ((Weight of Crucible + Coal - Weight of Crucible after Heating) / (Weight of Crucible + Coal)) x 100

Experiment No. 6: Redwood Viscometer Viscosity Calculation

Aim: To determine the kinematic viscosity and absolute viscosity of the given lubricating oil at different temperatures using Redwood Viscometer.

Calculation:

The kinematic viscosity is calculated using the following formula:

            Kinematic Viscosity = Time Taken (Redwood Seconds) x Oil Density

Experiment No. 7: Aniline Point of Lubricating Oil

Aim: To determine the aniline point of a given sample of lubricating oil, which helps evaluate the oil’s aromatic content and predict its solubility and compatibility with other materials.

Calculation:

Formula: Aniline Point = Temperature (°C) at which oil and aniline mix completely.
